Types of Scams :

üìß Phishing Scams

Description:Phishing is when scammers impersonate legitimate companies (like banks, online stores, or email providers) and trick people into clicking fake links, downloading harmful attachments, or entering sensitive information (like passwords or credit card numbers) on fake websites.

Examples:
  • A fake email from "your bank" saying your account is locked and asking you to login immediately.
  • A message claiming you've won a reward and need to verify your identity.
How to Stay Safe:
  • Always verify links and email senders.
  • Don‚Äôt click suspicious links.
  • Banks and companies never ask for credentials over email or SMS.

üî¢ OTP Fraud / SIM Swap

Description:Scammers call or message pretending to be a bank representative or company executive. They convince you to share OTPs (One-Time Passwords) or install apps that give them remote access to your device. This is often used to bypass 2FA (two-factor authentication).

Examples:
  • A scammer says your account needs verification and asks for the OTP you just received.
  • SIM Swap: Scammer tricks your mobile provider into issuing a new SIM for your number, gaining control of your SMS and calls.
How to Stay Safe:
  • Never share OTPs or PINs with anyone.
  • Do not install apps from unknown sources.
  • Use a strong screen lock and secure your phone number.

üõçÔ∏è Online Shopping Scams

Description:These scams involve fake websites or social media ads offering products at unbelievable prices. Once you pay, either the product never arrives or it's of extremely poor quality.

Examples:
  • Instagram or Facebook ad selling branded shoes at 90% off.
  • Websites with no customer reviews or contact info offering electronics at very low rates.
How to Stay Safe:
  • Avoid deals that look too good to be true.
  • Check reviews, refund policies, and contact details.
  • Prefer trusted e-commerce platforms.

üíº Job Offer Scams

Description: Scammers post fake job openings or send fake job offer letters, often promising high salaries and asking for "registration" or "training" fees in advance.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Verify job offers from official websites or HR contacts.
  • Watch out for poor grammar or unofficial email domains.

üîç How Scams Work

Scams follow a step-by-step process where scammers trick you into giving them your trust, information, or money. They use technology, fake identities, and psychological tricks to manipulate their victims.

How scammer do scams

  1. Set the Trap : Scammer creates a fake offer, message, job, or website.
  2. Target the Victim : Sends fake email, SMS, WhatsApp, or makes a call.
  3. Gain Trust : Pretends to be a bank official, government officer, friend, or company.
  4. Create Pressure : Uses urgency: ‚ÄúAct fast or account will be blocked!‚Äù
  5. Collect Sensitive Info : Asks for OTP, passwords, card numbers, or money.
  6. Steal & Escape : Once data or money is received, they vanish or block you.
